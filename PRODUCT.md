# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: Israelis preparing to file or appeal a disability claim with Bituach Leumi (the National Insurance Institute), who want to self-estimate their disability percentage before engaging further. Many are older adults or people managing an active health condition, so they're often stressed, unfamiliar with the bureaucratic process, and reading in a state of anxiety about money and their diagnosis.

## Product Purpose

Mimushon makes the official Bituach Leumi disability-percentage formula usable by ordinary people: a claimant selects their medical impairments and severities, and the calculator walks through the same weighted calculation the medical committee would use, showing every step rather than a black-box number. It exists to close the gap between "I have a diagnosis" and "I know roughly what percentage I'm likely to be awarded, and why." Success means a claimant leaves with a number they trust because they can see how it was built, and with enough confidence to take the next step — filing, appealing, or seeking further help.

## Positioning

Accuracy to the real process. Mimushon mirrors the actual official calculation the medical committee performs, step by step, rather than offering a generic or opaque estimate.

## Brand Personality

Plain-spoken and practical. No polish for its own sake — trust comes from clarity, directness, and getting the claimant to a number fast, not from persuasion or reassurance theater. Confident but not clinical-cold; official-feeling in its accuracy, not in its tone.

## Anti-references

Not a generic SaaS/startup look — no gradient-hero, glassmorphic cards, or product-marketing gloss; this isn't software being sold. Not a cold government form — dense, gray, unfriendly, bureaucratic. Not a pushy legal-firm sales site — aggressive CTAs, stock photos of gavels and handshakes, personal-injury-ad energy.

## Design Principles

- Show the work: every screen should make the calculation legible, not just the result — this is the core trust mechanism, not a nice-to-have.
- Plain over polished: prefer the direct, practical layout over decorative flourish; if a design choice doesn't help someone reach their number faster or trust it more, cut it.
- Respect the moment: users arrive stressed about health and money — keep flows calm, unhurried, and free of urgency tactics or dark patterns.
- One tool, two jobs: the same page must both stand alone as a trustworthy resource and gently move someone who wants more help toward contact — never let the sell overpower the tool.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA. Hebrew RTL throughout. Given a claimant base skewing toward older adults and people managing active health conditions, favor larger touch targets, simple linear flows, and minimal reliance on hover or gesture, even though no accommodation beyond AA was explicitly requested.
